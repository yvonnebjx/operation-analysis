# Seasonality Knowledge

Use this file to avoid treating normal seasonal cost shifts as unexplained anomalies.

## Why seasonality matters

Cross-border ecommerce economics often change around major promotional periods. A cost increase may be abnormal in one month and normal in another.

## Common seasonal effects

### Promotional seasons

- Prime Day
- Back to school
- Black Friday / Cyber Monday
- Christmas / year-end

These periods often cause:

- higher advertising costs
- stronger discount pressure
- temporary conversion improvement or deterioration depending on competition
- different channel-mix behavior

### Storage and inventory effects

- high inventory may have limited impact in normal periods
- the same inventory can become a major profit issue in peak periods because storage fees and turnover pressure increase
- overseas warehouse cost spikes may be structurally seasonal rather than purely operational

## Interpretation rules

- when advertising rate rises, check whether the month is a major platform campaign period before calling it abnormal
- when storage or overseas warehouse cost rises, check whether the business is entering peak storage pricing windows
- when discount rate deepens in a large promotion month, ask whether margin sacrifice translated into GMV or profit improvement
- if seasonal cost increases happen without corresponding scale gain, they are more likely to be real operating risks
