# Channel Knowledge

This file contains cross-border ecommerce background knowledge that matters before drawing operating conclusions.

## Core idea

Different channels may show similar revenue lines but very different cost structures. Conclusions should not assume that all channels behave like Amazon SC.

## Common channels

### SC

- usually means Amazon Seller Central
- merchant-side operating model
- common fee subjects: advertising, platform commission, FBA/FBM delivery, storage, refund loss
- price and traffic control are usually more direct than VC

### VC

- usually means Amazon Vendor Central
- retailer / wholesale-style relationship
- margin logic often differs from SC because pricing power, trade terms, and promotional mechanics differ
- when a model is bad in VC but not SC, the issue may be channel-commercial rather than product-only

### Walmart / Marketplace-like channels

- fee structures can resemble SC but conversion, refund, and delivery characteristics may differ
- do not assume Amazon benchmarks apply directly

### TikTok Shop / DTC

- often more sensitive to advertising intensity, content efficiency, and discounting
- structure shifts into these channels can increase revenue while hurting gross profit

### Offline / special channels

- may have very different cost recognition logic
- do not mix directly with marketplace channels without checking field definitions

## Interpretation rules

- if a problem exists across multiple channels for the same model, suspect product economics, pricing, or refund behavior first
- if a problem exists only in one channel, suspect channel structure, platform fee logic, promotional mechanics, or fulfillment setup
- commission changes should usually be interpreted as channel-mix or refund-related first, not as a standalone operational failure
